Interesting Information About Bearded Dragons

What is a Bearded Dragon?

The scientific name is Pogona.
They are a cold-blooded reptile, a lizard.

Why are they called a bearded dragon?

They have an ability to puff out the skin
in the throat area, the beard.

Can they change color?

Yes, they can darken their scales when they are angry or frightened and also, to soak up more light.

Where are they from?

They are orginally from Australia.

What do they eat?

They eat plants and insects.

How big do they get?

They grow up to 24 inches long.

Do they make good pets?

They are very good pets.
They are easy to care for and
they are very docile.

More Bearded Dragon Information
Average Lifespan 8-12 years (captivity)
Maximum Length 24 inches
Average Weight between 0.6 & 1.1 lbs.
Reproduction lay eggs
Natural Predators Birds
Venomous Venom is harmless to Humans
